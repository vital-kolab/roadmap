Platform architecture

A submission, benchmarking, and communication layer for translational autism research.

ROADMAP connects neural hypothesis submissions, dataset submissions, evidence review, and community feedback in one structured workflow. The goal is to make autism research claims easier to test, compare, explain, and responsibly move toward clinical or product-facing translation.

How ROADMAP works

Every contribution becomes part of a traceable evidence record.

1

Submit

Models, datasets, tasks, clinical questions, or community feedback enter the platform.

2

Map

Each item is linked to constructs, species, measures, assumptions, and relevant claims.

3

Benchmark

Models and datasets are compared using transparent criteria and known limitations.

4

Review

Evidence cards summarize confidence, uncertainty, and translation readiness.

5

Communicate

Outputs are tailored to clinicians, researchers, developers, and community members.

Core modules

Public platform components

DS

Dataset registry

Metadata, access conditions, task descriptions, participant context, measures, and analysis notes.

MR

Hypothesis registry

Model architecture, training objective, code links, benchmark results, predictions, and known failures.

EC

Evidence cards

Structured summaries of claims, evidence sources, uncertainty, readiness, and next validation steps.

CF

Community feedback

Questions, priority-setting, language review, and feedback on research or product-facing claims.

BR

Benchmark runner

Standardized tests for model-data fit, generalization, cross-species comparison, and failure modes.

TR

Translation readiness

Clear labels for exploratory results, research-ready claims, trial-planning evidence, and unsupported use.

Product direction

ROADMAP should launch as a professional web platform where the public landing page explains the mission, each portal supports concrete submissions or requests, and every output preserves evidence, uncertainty, and accountability.
