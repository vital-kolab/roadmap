Knowledge User Hub

From benchmarked evidence to carefully scoped tools.

ROADMAP does not only organize benchmarks. It creates a path for selected neural hypotheses, datasets, and model results to become reviewed knowledge products: explanations, evidence cards, task aids, and deployable prototypes for clinicians, researchers, families, educators, product partners, and community organizations.

Knowledge users

Who is this hub for?

Different users need different products. The same benchmarked evidence can be rendered as a clinician-facing summary, a research design aid, a community explanation, or a product-integration concept.

C

Community members and families

Request plain-language explanations, review claims for clarity and respect, and help identify which outputs feel useful, concerning, or irrelevant.

CL

Clinicians and educators

Review evidence cards, uncertainty summaries, and research-supported task suggestions that can inform discussion or research planning.

R

Researchers

Convert benchmark-supported hypotheses into new tasks, stimulus sets, experimental controls, or intervention-relevant predictions.

P

Product and platform partners

Explore whether a reviewed ROADMAP output is mature enough for pilot deployment, usability testing, or integration into an existing platform.

Evidence-to-tool pipeline

How ROADMAP moves from benchmark results to deployable knowledge products.

This pipeline protects against over-claiming. A result does not become a tool because it scored well once. It becomes a candidate tool only after scope, evidence, limitations, and user relevance are reviewed.

1

Submission

Behavioral data, neural hypotheses, or simulation requests enter ROADMAP with metadata and provenance.

2

Benchmarking

Results are tested against behavioral domains, source-linked datasets, and Brain-Score collaboration outputs when relevant.

3

Selection

Promising results are nominated as candidate tools only when evidence is strong enough for a defined purpose.

4

Review

Scientific, clinical, and community advisors check claims, uncertainty, usability, and wording.

5

Prototype

The result becomes a scoped explanation, evidence card, task selector, or deployable partner module.

6

Feedback loop

Knowledge-user feedback returns to ROADMAP and updates benchmark cards, summaries, and future submissions.

Deployable tool types

What could be deployed?

ROADMAP tools should be narrow, explainable, and reviewable. They are supports for interpretation and communication, not clinical diagnoses or medical advice.

Explanation

Plain-language explanation tools

Turn a technical hypothesis or benchmark result into a short explanation of what is known, what is uncertain, and what the result does not claim.

Evidence

Clinician and researcher evidence cards

Summaries of datasets tested, benchmark domains covered, confidence, limitations, and translation-readiness level.

Task design

Task and stimulus recommendation aids

Recommend benchmarked tasks, behavioral domains, or stimuli that best test a neural hypothesis or candidate model.

Pilot

Prototype modules for partners

Selected, reviewed outputs may become pilot modules for knowledge platforms, research apps, or deployment environments.

Reflection

Journaling or tracking interpretation aids

Carefully bounded tools could help organize patterns in task, environment, or support-related data when validated and reviewed.

Safety

Decision supports, not diagnoses

ROADMAP deployable tools are not substitutes for clinical judgment, medical care, diagnosis, or individualized support planning.

Deployment readiness

Not every benchmark result should become a tool.

ROADMAP should label outputs by readiness level so knowledge users know whether they are seeing an early idea, a benchmark-supported claim, or a pilot-ready prototype.

Concept

Scientifically plausible, but not yet benchmarked.

Benchmark-supported

Tested against one or more ROADMAP domains or source-linked datasets.

Reviewed

Scientific, clinical, and community advisors have checked scope and wording.

Pilot-ready

Ready for limited usability testing with knowledge users.

Partner-ready

Ready for careful co-development or integration into an approved platform.

Boundary statement

ROADMAP tools support communication and research translation.

They do not provide diagnosis, treatment recommendations, or individualized medical advice. Every deployable output should preserve evidence, uncertainty, limitations, and review status.
