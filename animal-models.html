Animal-model hypothesis portal

Submit neural hypotheses emerging from animal-model studies.

This portal is for animal-model researchers whose experiments reveal candidate neural mechanisms for autistic behavior, sensory processing, learning, social communication, or intervention response. ROADMAP treats these findings as testable hypotheses that can be linked to human datasets, simulations, and clinical research questions.

What gets submitted

Not “a model” alone — a hypothesis with evidence, scope, and limits.

01

Animal-model finding

Describe the assay, species, manipulation, behavioral phenotype, and neural measurement that motivated the hypothesis.

02

Candidate mechanism

State the neural mechanism: for example sensory reliability, E/I balance, feedback modulation, learning dynamics, or circuit-level variability.

03

Translational prediction

Specify what should be observable in human behavior, neuroimaging, clinical tasks, or simulations if the hypothesis is useful.

Benchmarking criteria

Every hypothesis is evaluated by what it predicts across evidence layers.

1
Evidence source

Which animal-model study, assay, or manipulation supports the neural hypothesis?

2
Cross-species prediction

What measurable signature should appear in human datasets, neurotypical simulations, or clinical research tasks?

3
Limits and risks

What does the hypothesis not claim? Which species, behaviors, or contexts should not be overinterpreted?

Neurotypical model simulation is handled through Brain-Score collaboration.

ROADMAP will not duplicate Brain-Score-style benchmarking infrastructure. Instead, neurotypical model simulations can be routed through a Brain-Score collaboration workflow, while ROADMAP focuses on autism-relevant hypotheses, datasets, cross-species evidence links, and translation-readiness summaries.

Scope and limits

Every submitted hypothesis should include what it does not claim.

Species limits

Which animal-model findings should not be directly generalized to people without additional evidence?

Behavioral limits

Which autistic behaviors, traits, or experiences are outside the scope of this hypothesis?

Translation limits

What additional human data, simulations, or clinical studies are needed before the hypothesis is used outside research?
